About Me

Hi! My name is Elizabeth Cho and I am currently a senior at Farmington High School in Farmington, Connecticut and I will be attending Johns Hopkins in the fall of 2018. I plan on majoring in biomedical engineering and computer science.

I first got involved with computers and technology in general when I was a child. My brother was known as the “tech support” of the house and I was fascinated by whatever he did, even if it was just formatting a Word document. When I got to high school, I took the computer science courses offered and joined the programming committee on the robotics team. From my experiences with learning the languages and working with others to solve problems, I decided that I wanted to go into this field. I also became interested in the field of biomedical engineering after taking biology and learning about some of the technological improvements made in research and treatment. After taking classes and teaching myself, I am able to write code in Java, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP.

Over the past few years, I have worked on several projects, both for classes and just because. A list of my main projects can be found here. The majority of my work is in Java, such as a program to encrypt messages for AP Computer Science and a model for semi-automated triage for AP Biology. Recently, I have started to expand to other languages. Currently, my main project is my capstone, where I’m researching how digital media can help with publicity. With the information I gather from this research, I will make an online choose-your-own-adventure game, which will be advertised through its development and release.
