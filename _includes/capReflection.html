Mid-Year Presentation Reflection

In this presentation, I was able to maintain a good pace throughout, although there was a moment where I had to wait a few seconds before the slide changed. In terms of speaking, I didn't speak too quickly, which allowed me to compose my thoughts as I went through the presentation. I was on the quieter side, but this could have been attributed to the fact that I was sick the day I was presenting. I did use crutch words and stutter at a couple of places throughout, which was due to the fact that I did feel a little nervous as I was presenting. One thing I noticed with my gestures was that I tend to use my left hand more than my right hand. The main thing I can do to improve for my final presentation is practice several times prior to the presentation date because I was comfortable with the content I was talking about and I did not feel like I had to spend an excessive amount of effort memorizing what I had to say. I did have notes with me, but only had to glance down at them occasionally.

Visually, the presentation was simple and clear with little text on the slides, but because of the theme I used, some of the text was difficult to read from the audience. In my final presentation, I will need to make sure there is enough contrast between the text and the background so it is readable from a distance.

Capstone Reflection and Advisor Meetings: Quarter 2

In November and December, I continued with my research by looking at additional resources, which included some abstracts to papers. I also worked on developing the game. When I met with my advisor, we discussed better ways to manage my time and prioritize my tasks, which involved listing out everything I had to do and sorting them based off of Randy Pausch's method of priority/not a priority and long-term/short-term. We also looked at some draft emails and questions I wrote for my community connections and interviews.

In January, I met with my advisor to talk about my progress in the research and contacting process. Although I have reached out to Pixelberry, I have yet to get a response, so I will need to spend more time on finding a more direct method of contacting them instead of using their support email. Some more time during this meeting was spent on discussing the public speaking aspect of my project. In my design plan, one of my goals was to improve my communication skills because I knew that this is one of the main weaknesses I have. A couple of options we discussed to progress in this area included reading a book about public speaking, attending classes or clubs dedicated to giving spontaneous talks, and asking others in the school or community for advice.

Over the past couple of months, I have been taking mental notes about what works for me in terms of public speaking to identify areas of improvement. I made these observations while doing presentations, such as the mid-year presentation, and while talking to the robotics team during meetings. Some things that I have noticed were that I am less stressed out when I am well-prepared for a presentation, but also when I don't care as much about what I am presenting, despite the fact that it seems counterintuitive. By knowing these things, I am more aware of what makes me more comfortable in these types of situations.

However, I have also noticed that I have an aversion to sending emails to people I am not familiar with, which leads to me putting it off for as long as I can. To deal with this, it seems like the only way is to send the email and get it over with. Another obstacle is managing my time throughout the rest of the year. I had thought that I would have more time after I was done with the college admissions process in December, but I was met with other tasks and events, such as build season in robotics and a lot of work in my other classes. This is where I would have to stick to the schedule I create for myself to ensure that I can complete everything I have to do while gettin enough slee, but also communicating when I am having trouble with managing what I have to do to see if there are other more efficient ways of doing things.

To complete my project by the end of the year, one of the main things I need to do is create social media accounts, which would include Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram, as well as accounts on additional appropriate platforms. Rather than delegating the task of managing the accounts to someone else, which was my initial plan, I will control the accounts, which makes more sense in the context of the focus of my research over the past several months. I also plan on contacting more people to complete my research.

The other main component of my project is the game I will be advertising. So far, I have written out the plot of the story and I am currently working on both the layout and the mechanics behind the game. I plan on maintaining contact with Stan Kamenskiy, who has given me advice on data storage, as well as referring to forums such as StackOverflow to figure out simpler problems.
