Reflections

September Advisor Meetings

I have met with my advisor a couple of times and topics discussed included the general idea and path for my research and final product and potential next steps, such as ideas for community connections. Following the networking conference, the idea of recruiting a "support team" was also mentioned, where I would delegate some of the parts of the final product to others who were willing to help me, rather than attempting to manage every single aspect of my planned final project and balance my schoolwork and extracurriculars.

Capstone Networking Conference: September 26, 2017

At this conference, I met with a professional who did work in a field similar to my research topic, who happened to work on more of the business side of the idea I had. One main takkeaway I got from this event was the idea of recruiting other students for a team so I could focus more on the coding part of the project and I could delegate art and marketing to other people. I added this tip to my design plan and allocated time to look for who might be a good fit for the role of marketing.

Advisor Meeting: October 13, 2017

I had this meeting mainly to discuss my design plan, which included going over it and seeing what could be added and improved. After this meeting, I added approximate deadlines for each step in the task list and explitly listed out who my specific resources for each step.

Advisor Meeting: October 20, 2017

This was a shorter meeting and what was mainly discussed was how I would approach my goal of improving my public speaking skills. I initially planned on practicing with robotics meetings because I often have to stand up in front of the team to talk about current projects and upcoming events. However, it was suggested that I find someone who I could specifically talk to so I could work on my public speaking skills because I wanted to work on both speaking in front of a larger audience and one-on-one with professionals.

Capstone Design Plan

I spent a considerable amount of effort in writing out the steps and thinking of who to ask as references. Rather than saving all of the work for last, I worked on it during class and at home and took time to meet with my advisor for advice on where it could be improved. I also got more ideas for who I could talk to for help because I don't know too many people who specialize in web development. While working on the design plan, I thought of the specific steps I would typically need to go through when working on a software project, such as back-end design, front-end design, and debugging. I also went through what I typically had to go through in a day when coming up with information about potential obstacles.
